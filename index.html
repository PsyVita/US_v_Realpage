Part II

The Plaintiff's Case

Sherman Act §§ 1 & 2
Market Definition · P1

The Hyper-Local Reality

  • Granular Market Definition: Rental housing competition operates strictly on a localized, neighborhood-by-neighborhood basis.
  • High Submarket Concentration: Within target institutional multifamily submarkets, the data-pooling network encompasses 70% to 90% of available supply.
  • The SSNIP Test Standard: Low cross-elasticity of demand proves luxury high-rises constitute a distinct product market from single-family homes.
  • Pre-Leasing Network Capture: New construction is integrated into the algorithmic pool prior to opening, establishing a uniform pricing baseline from day one.
Causation · P2

The Algorithmic Premium & Market Contagion

  • The Isolated Premium: Advanced regression models isolate a statistically significant “RealPage Premium” over non-software properties.
  • Umbrella Pricing Mechanism: Artificially inflated benchmarks pull the entire market baseline upward, affecting non-users.
  • Systemic Suppression of Concessions: Software protocols explicitly eliminate traditional discounts (e.g., “one month free”) to enforce downward price rigidity.
Operational Mechanics · P3

Coordinated Supply Suppression

  • Horizontal Competitor-Data Pooling: The platform relies entirely on the exchange of nonpublic, real-time competitor data.
  • Horizontal Lease Staggering: Algorithmic coordination of lease expirations acts as a functional market-allocation scheme.
  • Revenue Protection Mode: Systemic suppression of the competitive instinct to lower rents, choosing vacancy to protect high price floors.
Class Certification · P4

Formulaic Injury & The Comcast Model

  • Formulaic Injury & Commonality: Liability rests on a systemic, common distortion of the baseline starting price under Rule 23(b)(3).
  • Comcast v. Behrend Compliance: Hedonic regression models perfectly isolate the software-driven premium from individual unit variables.
  • Propensity Score Matching: Control benchmarks utilize mathematically paired markets to isolate the “but-for” competitive baseline.
